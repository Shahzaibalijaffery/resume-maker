ATS Resume Guide: Build a Resume That Gets Parsed and Shortlisted

Applicant Tracking Systems scan resumes before recruiters see them. An ATS-friendly resume improves parsing accuracy, keeps your work experience readable, and helps your profile rank higher for role-specific keywords.

Core ATS Rules

  • Use standard section names: Summary, Experience, Education, Skills, Projects.
  • Avoid complex tables, text boxes, and decorative visual elements.
  • Match role keywords from the job description naturally in context.
  • Prefer clean fonts, clear spacing, and consistent date formatting.
  • Export to PDF only after verifying text is selectable and searchable.

Best Template Choice for ATS

Single-column layouts are generally safest for broad ATS compatibility, including older systems. Two-column layouts can look excellent for modern ATS but may reduce parsing reliability on legacy parsers.

How to Optimize Your Resume Content

  1. Write a 2-4 line summary aligned to target role requirements.
  2. Use measurable achievement bullets in experience (impact, metric, scope).
  3. Add role-relevant technical skills and tools.
  4. Include projects that show practical outcomes and technology depth.
  5. Review the final PDF before every application submission.
